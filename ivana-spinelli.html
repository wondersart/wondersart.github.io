Ivana Spinelli

About the Artist

Ivana Spinelli (b. 1972) is living and working between Berlin and Bologna where she is teaching sculpture since 2012, most recently at Accademia di Belle Arti di Bologna. Her artistic practice spanning over two decades is revolving around analysis of the relationships between body and language and their ability to continuously shift the limits of the perceived and reality by redefining each other over and again.

Ivana Spinelli’s long-term research unfolds in the everlasting space of tension where normativity of the legislative language, of brand communication and social media meet a vital social and political dimension that reacts through protest and critical re-appropriation of the world.

Strict definitions of clandestine, female, vegetable, minimum, guerilla are twisted and rendered open through sculptures, installations, drawings, but also through actions such as sewing, wearing, translating, giving voice to others often in performances and workshops.

Ivana Spinelli

Zig Zag Protofilosofia digital series

Price: 3.000 Euro

What are these mysterious artworks?

The proto prefix in the name of the series is hinting to ancient wisdom, the esthetics to something in the future – could that be codes of A.I. communication?

As both archeology and science fiction are producing scripts, speculations and meaning, Spinelli arranges such different approaches {looking backwards} and {looking forward} under one story. On the one hand she is unearthing elements from «language of the Goddess», a long-lost prehistoric sign language of a matriarchal society rediscovered by Lithuanian anthropologist Marija Gimbutas. On the other hand on that precious legacy of sisterhood she is building an original code, a generative language of zigzags and related forms, decorative at first sight, yet more than able to contain a new discourse.
