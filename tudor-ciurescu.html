About the Artist

Tudor Ciurescu (b.1996, Craiova, Romania) is a visual artist currently living, working and studying (MA program at Ecole Cantonale d’Art Lausanne) in Lausanne, Switzerland.

Image-and-object-making stand at the core of his artistic practice. Delving in different streams of research and narratives coming from art history, economy, science fiction and digital sociology, Tudor Ciurescu is offering us surprising renderings of such explorations.

An indirect, yet in-depth and accurate representation of the contemporary and digital generation, his works are navigating numerous subjects from memes to critical reflection about bitcoin, from crushing anxieties and cunning strategies to deal with them to different stimulations and simulation in coming-of-age stories. The playfulness of his carefully chosen imagery, conveyed through multiple mediums, adds to the new visual languages younger people have created through trends, brands and visual matter in the cyber world.

Selected shows: Young Artist Connections, Museum of Contemporary Art, Bucharest (RO), Out Of Blue, Museum of Recent Art, Bucharest (RO), Le Temps et Moi - Musée Romain de Lausanne-Vidy (CH), ‘Still Alive’, CAV Gallery, Bucharest (RO, solo), ‘‘Open Patterns’’, Galeria Electroputere, Craiova (RO), ’Meet Your Critics’, Kreuzberg Pavilion, Berlin (DE), CameraPlus Photography Biennale Iasi (RO), ‘Aumaumuo’, Oumuamua, Amsterdam (NL), Salon ACME 9, New Mexico (MX), Sculpture Garden, Basel, Switzerland (CH).

Public collections: Museum Of Recent Art Bucharest (RO)

Besides his artistic practice, in 2016 he co-founded the creative studio Moxa20 which currently stands as a cultural point in Bucharest and in 2019 the art gallery Goodbuy, in Bucharest, a propitious space for intercultural exchange and experimentation for emerging artists.

You Taught Me How To Draw Stars

Price: 3.000 Euro

You Taught Me How To Draw Stars succeeds the impossible: to be a solid reflection on cryptocurrency mining in the present, future and past aswell.

It is the gold rush of this age with all the potentialities and failures brought into conversation by Tudor Ciurescu; and the poetic title unfolds the feeling of excitement this monolithic structure gave the artist through its technological features.

The physical object takes the core elements of a standard cryptocurrency miner and replicates them in the same dimensions through materials such as marble, plastic, aluminum and led. Transformed into a design artefact, the work questions the modes of presenting, explaining and collecting in the future this type of machine – that in 30 years will be simultaneously obsolete and iconic.

The physical version of this work was presented in solo show Still Alive – CAV Bucharest

I Sucked At Being A Kid

Price: 1.800 Euro

Surprising, edifying and quite moving autofictional snippet, I Sucked At Being A Kid uncovers a path back to a precocious coming-of-age process imbued in dark fiction, SF psychedelia and ghostly computer games.

An initial still version of this work was presented in solo show Still Alive – CAV Bucharest
